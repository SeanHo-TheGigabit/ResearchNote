## â“ Frequently Asked Questions

### Why do I get sick if I have an immune system?
Your immune system is amazing but not perfect. Pathogens evolve to evade it, and your immune system takes time to respond (days for first infection). This is why vaccines help - they train your immune system in advance!

### Why do I get the same cold multiple times?
The "common cold" is caused by >200 different viruses. Each is unique, so immunity to one doesn't protect against others. Also, some viruses (like flu) mutate rapidly.

### What is autoimmunity?
When your immune system attacks your own tissues. Normally, self-reactive cells are deleted (negative selection), but sometimes this fails or tolerance breaks down.

### How do vaccines work?
Vaccines show your immune system a "picture" of a pathogen (weakened virus, protein fragment) without causing disease. Your immune system responds, creates memory cells, and can fight the real pathogen quickly if you encounter it later.

### Why do organ transplants get rejected?
Your immune system recognizes "non-self" via MHC molecules. Donor organs have different MHC â†’ your T cells recognize as foreign â†’ attack. This is why MHC matching and immunosuppression are needed.

### Can you boost your immune system?
The immune system is a balanced system - too little activity = infections, too much = autoimmunity/allergies. "Boosting" isn't always good. Best support: vaccines, sleep, nutrition, exercise, stress management.
